5 ways to use social media for customer service

Almost every business has a social media presence these days. And for good reason, social media is a powerful way to connect with customers and build relationships. Now more than ever people are connected and they love connecting with brands wherever they are! But many businesses are failed to create two-way communication with the users. They just use it to blast out marketing messages. There is much more to do instead of just throwing content.

64% of consumers want brands to establish a personal connection with them. So, it's quite a big percentage to realize that brands should be focusing on connecting with the customers and providing customer service on social media. Customer service is the support you offer your customers to make their lives smoother. People not only want to just follow a brand but they also want to feel to be connected with them so they get listened to whenever they want any help. There are many examples where brands went ahead and responded to people on social media like humans. That's what actually customers want. They are humans so interact with them like that. Wendy's takes their social media seriously. They follow what people are talking about and use it to create an engaging experience for customers by listening on Twitter.

Here are 5 ways to harness the power of social media for customer services:

Respond with a personal touch

Whenever a user gets service from a brand he subconsciously starts building a relationship (can be good or bad) with it. And users do share their thoughts on social media sometimes in the form of reviews about how they feel about a specific product/service. Those review ratings are one of the metrics of your company's online reputation.

Review is not only just user satisfaction rating, social platforms do consider it seriously to show your service as a suggestion whenever it suits the user who is exploring the same category service. Replying to those reviews with a personal touch can go a long way and can make customers feel that their feedback is valuable, and heard. This can be another chance to make him feel special again and strengthen the relationship.

Engage to leave long-lasting impact

Social media is not a one way street, people do comment on brand posts, and leave message for discussion. Posting something on social media gives an opportunity to refresh brand image in users mind and a way to engage with the brands through comments. This is the right moment to express that brands do have souls and it's a human they are engaging with (not a robot that sends the same reply to everyone). Aligning the tone and voice of your content to drive engagement requires an in-depth understanding of who will be most interested. Use emoji, GIFs, or slang to drop the curtain for a friendly connection.

Be transparent about company processes

Being proactive in not only responding to incoming queries but also taking measures to notify them beforehand. Be honest and authentic as it really goes a long way. People do appreciate that and this also helps to tell them that you are aware of the issue and taking actions to make things better continuously. It's not hard to understand why--the more information you share with your audience, the better chance they have at understanding who YOU truly ARE and what makes YOUR company different from others out there.

Keep track of what people are saying about you

Something good happens, people talk on social media. Something bad happens, they talk on social media. There are a lot of reasons to use social media for customer service to make customers feel like their voice has weight. Brand mentions and hashtags monitoring can help to understand how customers perceive the image of your brand. You can keep an eye on social media and see what people are talking about you, which will give insight into whether or not they have positive feelings towards the company in general.

Tip:Share customer feedback — especially when it’s positive. By encouraging your customers to share their positive experiences with you, both internally and externally via social channels, it will help create a more engaged workforce that's excited about the brand.

Turn negative feedback into an opportunity

The report by Statista has revealed that 33% of users have used social media to complain about a brand or its customer service. The best thing about receiving negative feedback is that it's an opportunity to figure out what needs improvement. You can use this as a chance for self-reflection, and improve your relationship with customers by making changes to fix things up. You must have a voice that is compassionate and caring, with the ability to make your customers feel like they're important.

Waiting is never fun, Mikey! Tell us about your visit here: https://t.co/WhvwT8dw7y

The best way to address criticism is by taking action. Action speaks louder than words, and it's also more effective at demonstrating transparency with your company’s policies or practices in the long run because you can show people what changes are being made on an ongoing basis.

Conclusion

With social media, you can engage with customers, keep track of what they are saying about your company and even turn negative feedback into an opportunity. Social media is a powerful tool for customer service that will help you build relationships with your current and potential customers alike. Be human, empathetic, and authentic while responding on social media.
